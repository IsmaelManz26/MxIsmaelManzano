The Legends of Motocross

The best athletes in extreme sports

Motocross is more than just a sport; it’s a constant test of courage, skill, and endurance. Over the years, some riders have left an indelible mark on this discipline, taking it to new heights with their talent and dedication.

In this section, you'll discover the riders who have turned every jump and every curve into a legend, inspiring generations of athletes and fans around the world.

Jorge Prado

Jorge Prado García, born on January 5, 2001, in Lugo, Spain, is one of the most outstanding riders in the world of motocross.

Prado began competing at a young age and quickly showed exceptional talent. At 17, in 2018, he won his first world championship in the MX2 category, becoming the first Spaniard to achieve this feat.

In 2019, he retained his MX2 world champion title, establishing himself as a rising star in the sport. Later, he moved up to the MXGP category, where he continued to stand out with consistent victories and podium finishes, and in 2023, he was crowned world champion in this category.

Antonio Cairoli

Antonio Cairoli, born on September 23, 1985, in Patti, Italy, is one of the most successful motocross riders in history, boasting nine world titles. He has made a significant impact on the sport, competing in both the MX2 and MXGP categories.

Cairoli is renowned for his technical skills and adaptability to various terrains. Over nearly two decades, he has consistently excelled in races, showcasing his endurance and unique riding style, which has garnered him a loyal global fanbase.

His legacy continues to inspire young riders worldwide, proving that passion and hard work are essential for achieving success in motocross.

Achievements and Titles

Antonio Cairoli

10x World Motocross Champion

Ricky Carmichael

Ricky Carmichael, born on November 27, 1979, in Tallahassee, Florida, USA, is widely regarded as one of the greatest motocross riders of all time. With a total of 15 national championships and multiple professional motocross titles, he has earned the nickname "The GOAT" (Greatest of All Time).

Throughout his career, Carmichael dominated both the 250cc and 450cc categories, setting records for wins in numerous seasons. His aggressive style and technical skill on the bike helped him achieve unparalleled success.

After retiring from professional racing, Carmichael transitioned into a commentator and trainer, continuing to influence the next generation of riders. His legacy in the sport remains significant, inspiring aspiring motocross athletes worldwide.

Achievements and Titles

Ricky Carmichael

10x World Motocross Champion

Ashley Fiolek

Ashley Fiolek, born on January 22, 1990, in St. Augustine, Florida, USA, is one of the few women to achieve significant success in professional motocross.

Despite being deaf, Fiolek has shown that nothing can stop her passion for racing. She won the Women's Motocross Championship (WMX) in 2008 and became a world champion in 2010, solidifying her place in motocross history.

Fiolek is known not only for her skills on the track but also for her role as an advocate for inclusion and female representation in the sport. Her courage and dedication have inspired many young women to pursue their dreams in motocross.
